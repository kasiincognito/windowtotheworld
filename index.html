What Astronauts See

Astronauts aboard the ISS orbit Earth every ~90 minutes and help scientists track climate, disasters, and environmental change.

See Benefits

Weightlessness (NBL)

Arrow keys / WASD to apply thrust. Space resets to center. Small damping simulates tiny resistance so motion slows slightly over time.

Neutral Buoyancy Lab

Astronauts train underwater in the NBL to rehearse spacewalks. The movement & skills translate to robotics, medicine, and remote operations on Earth.

Benefits on Earth

Climate Monitoring


Satellites track weather, deforestation, and ice loss.

Microgravity research improves surgical tools and therapies.

Robotics


Space robotics transfers to industry and exploration tech.

Ocean Research


Observation supports oceanography and disaster response.
